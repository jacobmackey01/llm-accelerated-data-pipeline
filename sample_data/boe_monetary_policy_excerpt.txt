Bank of England Monetary Policy Summary
6 February 2025

The Monetary Policy Committee voted by a majority of 7-2 to reduce Bank Rate by 0.25 percentage points, to 4.50%.

Twelve-month CPI inflation was 2.5% in December 2024, above the 2% target. Services consumer price inflation remained elevated, although pay growth had shown signs of easing.

UK GDP growth was weaker than expected in the second half of 2024. Business surveys suggested subdued demand, while the labour market had continued to loosen gradually.

The Committee judged that a gradual and careful approach to removing monetary policy restraint remained appropriate. Monetary policy would need to continue to remain restrictive for sufficiently long until the risks to inflation returning sustainably to the 2% target had dissipated further.
